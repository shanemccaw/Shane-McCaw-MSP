# MSP RESELLER MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

**Shane McCaw Consulting**
*Microsoft 365 Governance, Monitoring & Reseller Platform*

This MSP Reseller Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is a binding contract between Shane McCaw Consulting ("Platform," "we," "us") and the managed service provider entity accepting these terms during onboarding ("MSP," "you"). This Agreement governs the MSP's access to and resale of the Platform's Microsoft 365 monitoring, governance, and consulting infrastructure. By checking the acceptance boxes below and completing onboarding, you agree to be bound by this Agreement.

This Agreement governs the Platform-to-MSP relationship only. It does not govern, and should not be read to govern, sales, billing, or support arrangements between an MSP and that MSP's own customers, which are the MSP's sole responsibility as set out in Section 5. Direct-to-customer services sold by Shane McCaw Consulting itself (outside the MSP reseller channel) are governed by a separate Terms of Service.

## 1. Definitions

- **"Platform"** means the Shane McCaw Consulting multi-tenant Microsoft 365 governance, monitoring, and consulting SaaS system, including all monitoring engines, signal-derivation logic, dashboards, and administrative tooling.
- **"MSP"** means the managed service provider entity that has registered as a reseller on the Platform.
- **"Customer"** means an end-client organization onboarded by the MSP to receive monitoring or related services through the Platform.
- **"Services"** means the monitoring infrastructure, signal engines, reporting, and wholesale catalog of products and add-ons made available to the MSP for resale.
- **"Wholesale Price"** means the price charged by the Platform to the MSP for a given product, subscription, or add-on, as published in the Platform's then-current MSP rate card.
- **"Resale Price"** means the price the MSP independently sets and charges its own Customer, which the Platform does not set, cap, or collect.

## 2. Scope of Services

The Platform grants the MSP a non-exclusive, non-transferable right to access the Platform's monitoring infrastructure and wholesale product catalog for the purpose of reselling monitoring, governance, and related services to the MSP's own Customers under a white-label model. The Platform provides the underlying infrastructure and wholesale access only; the MSP is responsible for its own customer relationships, sales, pricing, and invoicing as described in Section 5.

The Services may include, without limitation: Microsoft 365 tenant monitoring, drift and security signal detection, governance reporting, add-on products, micro-remediation services, document products, retainers, and project-based engagements, as made available in the Platform's catalog from time to time.

## 3. Tenant Eligibility

The Platform is designed and supported exclusively for commercial Microsoft 365 tenants. The Platform does not support, and the MSP shall not onboard, any tenant operating under Microsoft GCC, GCC High, DoD, or other U.S. Government Community Cloud environment. The MSP represents and warrants that each Customer tenant onboarded to the Platform is a commercial (non-government-cloud) Microsoft 365 tenant. Onboarding a tenant in violation of this Section is a material breach of this Agreement.

## 4. Payment Authorization & Billing

The Platform operates a single, centralized billing relationship with the MSP. The Platform does not process payments on behalf of, or collect payment information from, the MSP's Customers. All charges under this Agreement are made to the payment method the MSP designates and maintains on file with the Platform ("Card on File").

### 4.1 What Gets Charged to the MSP's Card on File

The MSP's Card on File will be charged, on a recurring monthly basis, for:

- (a) the MSP's own Platform subscription fee, at the tier selected by the MSP; and
- (b) Wholesale Price charges for any catalog product, subscription, add-on, or service initiated by the MSP's onboarded Customers through the Platform, regardless of whether the MSP individually reviews or approves each individual transaction, unless and until the MSP has enabled a Platform-provided purchase-approval control for its account.

Customers do not enter payment information into the Platform and are never charged directly by the Platform under this Agreement. All amounts owed by a Customer to the MSP for services resold by the MSP are invoiced and collected by the MSP directly, entirely outside the Platform, as described in Section 5.

### 4.2 Resale Pricing Is the MSP's Own Decision

The Platform charges the MSP at Wholesale Price only. The MSP has sole and unrestricted discretion to set the Resale Price it charges its own Customers, and the Platform has no visibility into, control over, or responsibility for that Resale Price.

### 4.3 Acknowledgment of Automated Purchases

Because Customers can initiate catalog purchases directly through the Platform, the MSP acknowledges that its Card on File may be charged automatically as a direct result of Customer activity the MSP did not individually initiate. The Platform will make commercially reasonable efforts to provide the MSP with purchase notifications, transaction records, and, where enabled, an approval-gate control; however, the MSP's authorization below applies regardless of whether such a control is enabled.

### 4.4 Failed Payments

If a charge to the Card on File fails, the Platform may suspend the MSP's account, suspend Customer-facing Services, and/or restrict new purchases until payment is resolved. The Platform is not responsible for any disruption to the MSP's own customer relationships resulting from a payment failure on the MSP's account.
